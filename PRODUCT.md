# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single user: the builder who treats their life like a system. Uses Goal OS daily — morning planning, task execution, habit tracking, budget review. Context: desktop, focused sessions, often early morning or late at night. Not a casual check-in app; this is the command center for the day.

## Product Purpose

A local-first personal operating system combining task scheduling, habit tracking, goal accountability, budget oversight, and identity-building into one disciplined daily interface. Success = the user feels in control of their time, energy, and direction every day they open it.

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Deliberate. Yours.

Voice is concise and action-oriented. No cheerful nudges, no gamification fluff. The interface respects the user's intelligence and treats them as someone running a system, not filling out a form.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: rounded blue cards, Tailwind defaults, dashboard-as-template. Looks like every startup tool.
- Google/Microsoft utility gray: enterprise-grade soullessness. Functional but dead.
- Habit tracker toy aesthetic: badge-heavy, streak-first, feels like a mobile game.

## Design Principles

1. **Command, not nudge.** The interface gives the user control. No encouraging copy, no celebration animations for routine tasks.
2. **Information density over whitespace theater.** Show what matters. Don't pad to look polished.
3. **Dark by default.** The user works at a desk, often early or late. Ambient light is low. The interface should match.
4. **Earned color.** Color signals state — priority, sync status, category. It is not decoration.
5. **Linear-quality without Linear's aesthetic.** Match the craft and precision, not the exact look. This is a personal OS, not a team tool.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigable. No motion for functional transitions unless reduced-motion is off. Color is never the sole indicator of state.
